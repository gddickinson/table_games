# Dragon Battle — Strategy Guide

## Party Composition & Roles

Your party of 6 has clear roles. Understanding them is key to victory.

### Front Line (Melee)
- **Kenji (Barbarian)**: Primary damage. Rage gives damage resistance — he should take hits so others don't. Use Reckless Attack when his HP is high.
- **Tomoe (Paladin)**: Secondary tank. Her Aura of Protection (+3 saves) protects nearby allies from breath weapon. Divine Smite on crits for massive burst.
- **Riku (Rogue)**: Melee flanker. Needs an ally adjacent to the dragon for Sneak Attack. Cunning Action lets him Disengage safely.

### Back Line (Ranged)
- **Yuki (Wizard)**: AoE damage. Fireball for adds (if any), Scorching Ray/Magic Missile for reliable single-target. Shield reaction keeps her alive.
- **Sora (Ranger)**: Sustained ranged DPS. Hunter's Mark + 2 attacks + Colossus Slayer = consistent damage every turn from safety.
- **Mei (Cleric)**: Healer. Position behind melee but within 60 ft. Cure Wounds the most damaged ally. Spirit Guardians if she ends up in melee.

## Opening Strategy (Rounds 1–3)

1. **Round 1**: Sora casts Hunter's Mark (bonus). Kenji Rages (bonus) and charges. Tomoe casts Shield of Faith on Kenji (bonus) then advances. Mei casts Spiritual Weapon (bonus). Riku hides (bonus) then moves to flank position. Yuki holds position at range.
2. **Round 2**: Melee engages. Sora and Yuki deal ranged damage. Mei heals if needed, otherwise Guiding Bolt.
3. **Round 3**: By now the dragon may use breath weapon. Absorb Elements (Sora), Shield (Yuki), Evasion (Riku). Mei heals breath damage.

## Breath Weapon Management

The dragon's Fire Breath deals an average of 63 fire damage (DC 21 DEX save for half). This is the deadliest attack.

- **Spread out**: Never cluster more than 2 characters together
- **After breath**: It must recharge (5–6 on d6). You have 1–2 turns of safety
- **Tomoe's Aura**: +3 to DEX saves. Stay within 10 ft of her
- **Sora's Absorb Elements**: Halves fire damage and adds 1d6 fire to her next melee
- **Riku's Evasion**: Success = 0 damage, fail = half damage. He's safest against breath

## Target Priority — When to Focus vs When to Spread

Always focus the dragon. There's only one enemy. The only question is WHO attacks and HOW.

### Damage Priority Per Character
1. **Kenji**: Always melee attack the dragon (Extra Attack = 2 attacks, Rage damage)
2. **Sora**: Always ranged attack the dragon (2 attacks + Hunter's Mark + Colossus Slayer)
3. **Riku**: Sneak Attack for burst damage, but only if he has advantage or flanking
4. **Tomoe**: Attack with Divine Smite on critical hits or when dragon is low HP
5. **Yuki**: Scorching Ray for single-target, save Fireball for if the dragon summons minions
6. **Mei**: Sacred Flame for chip damage, focus on healing unless everyone is healthy

## Healing Economy

Mei has limited spell slots. Use them wisely.

- **Cure Wounds** (1st level): 1d8+4 = avg 8.5 HP. Use on anyone below 50%.
- **Cure Wounds** (2nd level): 2d8+4 = avg 13 HP. Use on tanks below 30%.
- **Cure Wounds** (3rd level): 3d8+4 = avg 17.5 HP. Emergency only.
- **Tomoe's Lay on Hands**: 25 HP pool. Use 5-10 HP at a time for efficiency.
- **Don't overheal**: Healing a character from 90% to 100% wastes resources.
- **Let Kenji take damage**: His Rage resistance means 1 HP of healing = 2 HP effective.

## Spell Slot Conservation

### Priority Spell Slot Usage
| Level | Best Use | Avoid |
|-------|----------|-------|
| 1st | Cure Wounds, Shield, Hunter's Mark, Absorb Elements | Low-damage attacks when melee works |
| 2nd | Spiritual Weapon, Scorching Ray, Smite on crits | Smite on normal hits early in fight |
| 3rd | Fireball (if AoE needed), Spirit Guardians, big Cure Wounds | Using 3rd-level slots in first 3 rounds |

### General Rules
- Save at least one 1st-level slot for Shield (Yuki) and Absorb Elements (Sora) as reactions
- Mei should save one 2nd+ level slot for emergency healing
- Tomoe should save one slot for a finishing Divine Smite when dragon is low

- **Kenji & Tomoe**: Adjacent to dragon, opposite sides if possible
- **Riku**: Adjacent to dragon on a third side (enables Sneak Attack)
- **Mei**: 30 ft behind melee, within Cure Wounds range (60 ft with movement)
- **Yuki**: 60 ft back, behind cover (pillar), within Scorching Ray range (120 ft)
- **Sora**: Maximum longbow range (150 ft), elevated position if available

## Dragon Phase Tactics

### Phase 1 (Dragon HP 100–50%): Survive
- The dragon hits hardest when fresh. 3 legendary actions + multiattack + possible breath.
- Focus on setting up: buffs, marks, rage, positioning
- Heal reactively, don't waste slots on small chip damage

### Phase 2 (Dragon HP 50–25%): Push Advantage
- Dragon targets healers in this phase. Protect Mei!
- Tomoe: Use Compelled Duel to force dragon to attack her instead
- All-out damage. Use 2nd and 3rd level spell slots now.
- The dragon uses Wing Attack to reposition — be ready to re-engage

### Phase 3 (Dragon HP below 25%): Finish It
- Dragon is desperate and dangerous. Don't get cocky.
- Burn remaining spell slots on damage (Divine Smite, Scorching Ray, Fireball)
- Keep one heal in reserve in case someone goes down
- Focus fire. Every point of damage counts.

## Legendary Action Awareness

The dragon gets 3 legendary actions per round, used BETWEEN other characters' turns.

- **Tail Attack (1 action)**: Watch for it when someone is behind the dragon
- **Wing Attack (2 actions)**: Knocks prone and repositions. Stay spread to limit targets.
- **Detect (1 action)**: Reveals hidden Riku. Re-hide on his next turn.

Legendary actions refresh at the START of the dragon's turn, not the end.

## Key Combos

1. **Guiding Bolt + Sneak Attack**: Mei's Guiding Bolt gives advantage to next attack → Riku's Sneak Attack with guaranteed advantage
2. **Spirit Guardians + Compelled Duel**: Tomoe forces dragon to stay near her while Mei's Spirit Guardians deals 3d8 per turn
3. **Reckless Attack + Smite on Crit**: Kenji's Reckless gives advantage (double crit chance) → Tomoe Smites on crits for 4d8+2d8 burst
4. **Hunter's Mark + Colossus Slayer**: Sora deals 1d8+1d6+1d8+4 per hit = avg 17 damage per attack
5. **Absorb Elements + Melee Follow-up**: Sora takes half breath damage then adds 1d6 fire to her next sword attack
